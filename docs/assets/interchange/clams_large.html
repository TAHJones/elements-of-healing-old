CLAMS – is an acronym for:

  • C – Concomitant – Other symptoms connected to the presenting complaint.
  • L – Location – The location of the presenting complaint and whether it moves from one place to another.
  • A - Aetiology – The cause of or circumstances at the time when the presenting complaint began e.g. a shock or accident.
  • M – Modality – Anything that causes the presenting complaint to get better or worse e.g. heat, cold or movement.
  • S – Sensation – How the client actually experiences the complaint e.g. the pain is dull, sharp, stabbing, twisting or throbbing.
